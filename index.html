About Civil

Civil is a community tool built around one simple idea: your neighbors and your local government should be on the same page.

Think of it as a running list of things your neighborhood needs fixed — and a way to make sure the city actually sees it.

What Civil is for

✅ Actionable suggestions. Report something specific that can be investigated, prioritized, and addressed — a pothole, a broken signal, an unsafe crossing, a park that needs attention.
✅ Community signal. Upvote issues you care about too. The more votes an issue gets, the harder it is for officials to ignore. Your vote is your voice.
✅ Two-way communication. Local government departments can log in, post status updates, and tell the community what they're doing about it. No more shouting into the void.

What Civil is not

🚫 Not a place to vent. If a post doesn't include a clear, actionable suggestion, it doesn't belong here. Frustration is valid — but it's most powerful when it comes with a ask.
🚫 Not social media. Civil isn't for general discussion, opinions on politics, or anything that isn't directly tied to a community improvement. Keep it focused and keep it useful.
🚫 Not anonymous complaints. Every post is attached to an account. Own your suggestion. Good ideas don't need to hide.

How it works

1. Report — Submit an issue with a clear title, category, neighborhood, and location. Describe why it matters.

2. Vote — Upvote issues you've noticed too. A ranked list of community priorities is far more powerful than a single complaint.

3. Watch — Local officials respond with status updates directly on the issue. You'll see when something moves from Open → In Progress → Resolved.

Currently piloting in

Santa Monica and Venice. If this works the way we hope, there's no reason it should stop at neighborhood borders.
